# Module 05 — Voiceover System

## Objective
Define Hebrew narration rules, pacing, casting, and recording specifications.

## Confirmed Approach: Automatic Hebrew TTS

Voiceover is generated automatically — no human recording required.
The web interface synthesizes Hebrew speech from the script text and plays it
synchronized with the animation. The user only types the topic and downloads the result.

## Background Music
A soft children's instrumental track plays continuously under the voiceover.
Music is lower in volume than the voice — it supports, never competes.
Music starts with the animation and fades out at the end of the episode.

## Hebrew Language Rules
- Use spoken Hebrew, not written Hebrew
- Short sentences — as a child would speak
- No formal register
- Words every 4-year-old knows — no rare vocabulary
- Read every line aloud before finalizing

## Pacing Rules
- Slow and deliberate — 100–120 words per minute maximum
- Pause 0.5 seconds between sentences
- Pause 1 second between acts
- Never rush to fit timing — shorten script instead

## Hebrew TTS Requirements
- Voice: warm, clear, child-friendly Hebrew
- Speed: slow and deliberate — appropriate for ages 4–7
- Each scene's lines are synthesized and timed to match scene duration
- Child character lines: higher, younger-sounding voice
- Animal companion lines: warm, calm adult voice
- Key message scene: same voice as animal, slight emphasis

## Background Music Requirements
- Style: soft children's instrumental (gentle melody, no lyrics)
- Volume: significantly lower than voiceover at all times
- Timing: begins with episode start, fades out after final scene
- Mood: warm, playful, encouraging — never dramatic or intense
- Duration: loops or fades naturally to match total episode length (40–50 sec)

## Script-to-Voice Rules
- Write script exactly as it will be spoken
- Use punctuation to guide pacing (comma = short pause, period = full pause)
- Add [PAUSE] marker where 1-second pause is needed
- Avoid words ending in consonant clusters — hard to pronounce clearly in Hebrew

## Quality Check
Before finalizing voiceover:
1. Play at 0.75x speed — still clear?
2. Play with eyes closed — is the message understood?
3. Play to a child under 6 — do they follow?
All three must pass.
